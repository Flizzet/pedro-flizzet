Ammo Inferno

Ammo Inferno is a 2D pixel art arcade game designed for Android and iOS. Built with Java and LibGDX for cross-platform development and Gimp for pixel art graphics. The project took approximately 5 weeks to complete and distribute. The project has since gained a lot of attention from publishers. However, due to the nature of the project, we're not focusing on a full release process. This project functions solely as a representation of our skills, and is downloadable on the Google Play Store. "Uncommonly for a mobile shooter Ammo Inferno has an appreciably large settings menu that gives several different control schemes to choose from and the option to enable/disable individual graphical features. Shoot ’em up aficionados will get a kick out of this platforming style runner."

About

For over 6 years, I have been working with game development in every spare second of my time. I have a long list of resources and a wide range of skills. Being incredibly passionate about games and programming has driven me to develop every free chance I get. I’ve built games with Java, HTML5, C#, Swift, C, Objective-C and many other languages. I’ve worked with a number of frameworks and libraries including LibGDX, Slick2D, Phaser, LWJGL, and MonoGame. I have experience developing for mobile and desktop with both cross-platform development and web development.

While game development requires a strong knowledge of programming, you won’t have a good game without much more. This includes good art, planning, concepts, passion, diagrams, and a developer who understands more than just the code. To make sure of this, I’ve spent countless hours practicing video game design, level design, art styles, UML diagrams, store listing design, handdrawn concepts and the hundreds of different skills required to publish a full fledged game.
